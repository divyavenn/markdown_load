Hello! I’m Divya and this is bitwise arcadia, a blog about computer engineering and ML.

Learning engineering often feels cold, impersonal, and jargon heavy, just as a tech-filled future can feel cyberpunk and dystopian.

I don’t think tech has to be either of those things. I think it can be intuitive and beautiful to think about and build and use. Arcadia is a reference to semi-mythical landscape of lush nature and harmony - a sort of out-of-date poetic byword for a wild idyll. It’s also an homage to my favorite Tom Stoppard play, Arcadia, and one of my favorite characters of all time: the intuitive and romantic and mathematically brilliant Thomasina Coverly.

bitwise arcadia is my obscure way of saying that (1) that these guides will be short and intuitive (2) the journey towards harmonious and powerful tech will have to be taken step by step. Many good things will be forgotten but will inevitably be found again.

I’m not an expert, but I want to be one. I’ll be working on some cool projects and I’ll occasionally share my progress. I’ll also write about concepts/jargon that confused me when I was learning how to code, many of which I learned embarrassingly late.

As an old manager of mine used to say, if there’s something you’re confused about, others probably are too, so do them a favor and ask.
